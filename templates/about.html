About Me

I am a graduate student in the Robotics Institute at CMU advised by Dr. Martial Hebert. I collaborate closely with Dr. Shubham Tulsiani from Facebook AI Research.


I am interested in pursuing research at the intersection of computer vision, machine learning and robotics. My long term goal is to build intelligent robots that can perceive the world and interact with it seamlessly. I envision these robots to operate like scientists: observe, hypothesize, test, learn. This calls for learning actionable representations of the environment. Crucially, for such learning to scale, robots need to be endowed with mechanisms for self improvement i.e. learning without supervision. I want to design algorithms that can enable robots to predict, synthesize and make decisions from disparate streams of information, and adapt to a changing environment. My ongoing research can be broadly classified into two categories: 1) Learning parsimonious semantic representations of 3D scenes captured as point clouds. Combining the expressive power of deep learning as a function approximator with geometric priors to capture the inherent structure present in the point clouds is a very important step towards full 3D scene understanding. I am particularly interested in the cognitive phenomena of perceptual organization and grouping. 2) Tackling long-standing problems in computer vision with deep learning using only geometric consistency as self-supervision. My main focus has been on 3D reconstruction from multiple images without the onerous requirement of human supplied 3D supervision.


Prior to joining CMU, I worked with Dr. Dhruv Batra and Dr. Devi Parikh at Virgina Tech. My work focused at the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing, specifically the task of Visual Question Answering(VQA). Earlier, I completed my Bachelors in Computer Engineering from University of Mumbai, India. I have been fortunate to have gotten an opportunity to work with fantastic people throughout my academic journey and I hope to continue the same.
