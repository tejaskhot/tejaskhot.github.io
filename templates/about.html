About Me

I am a graduate student in the Robotics Institute at CMU advised by Dr. Martial Hebert

and work closely with Dr. Christoph Mertz as a part of the Navlab. I am also collaborating with Dr. Ruslan Salakhutdinov.

My ongoing research can be broadly classified into two categories: 1) Learning parsimonious semantic representations of 3D scenes captured as point clouds. Combining the expressive power of deep learning as a function approximator with geometric priors to capture the inherent structure present in the point clouds is a very important step towards full 3D scene understanding. 2) Learning behaviors for robot agents in 3D environments using deep reinforcement learning. I am interested in exploring the emergence of intelligent behavior from raw sensorimotor data and its multi-modal associations with memory, perception and action.



Prior to joining CMU, I worked with Dr. Dhruv Batra and Dr. Devi Parikh at Virgina Tech. My work focused at the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing, specifically the task of Visual Question Answering(VQA). Our work on addressing the strong language priors in the VQA dataset was accepted to CVPR 2017. Earlier, I completed my Bachelors in Computer Engineering from University of Mumbai, India. I have been fortunate to have gotten an opportunity to work with fantastic people throughout my academic journey and I hope to continue the same.
